    D. 16

    | Discuss

    Answer : Option D

    Explanation :

    Let the Cost Price (CP) of one article = 1

    => CP of x articles = x ------------------------------(Equation 1)



    CP of 20 articles = 20



    Given that cost price of 20 articles is the same as the selling price of x articles

    => Selling price (SP) of x articles = 20--------------(Equation 2)



    Given that Profit = 25%

    $MF#%

    \Rightarrow \left (\dfrac{SP - CP }{CP}\right ) = \dfrac{25}{100} = \dfrac{1}{4}\text{------------( Equation 3)}$MF#%



    


    
Substituting equations 1 and 2 in equation 3,

    $MF#%\begin{align}

    & \Rightarrow \dfrac{\left(20-x \right )}{x} = \dfrac{1}{4} \\ \\

    & \Rightarrow 80 - 4x = x \\ \\

    & \Rightarrow 5x = 80 \nonumber \\ \\

    & \Rightarrow x = \dfrac{80}{5} = 16 \\ \\

    \end{align} $MF#%



    3. If selling price is doubled, the profit triples. What is the profit percent?

    A. $MF#%100$MF#%

    B. $MF#%105\dfrac{1}{3}$MF#%

    C. $MF#%66\dfrac{2}{3}$MF#%

    D. $MF#%120$MF#%

    View Answer
    | Discuss

    Here is the answer and explanation

    Answer : Option A

    Explanation :

    Let the CP = x , SP = y

    profit = SP –CP = y-x



    If SP is doubled, SP = 2y

    Now Profit = SP –CP = 2y - x



    Given that If selling price is doubled, the profit triples

    $MF#%\begin{align}

    &\Rightarrow 2y – x = 3(y-x) \\ \\

    &\Rightarrow 2y – x = 3y - 3x \\ \\

    &\Rightarrow y = 2x \\ \\

    &\Rightarrow Profit\% = \dfrac{Profit}{CP} \times 100 \\ \\

    &= \dfrac{y-x}{x} \times 100 \\ \\&= \dfrac{2x – x }{x} \times 100 \\ \\&= \dfrac{x}{x} \times 100 \\ \\&= 100\% \\ \\

    \end{align}$MF#%



    4. In a shop, the profit is 320% of the cost. If the cost increases by 25% but the selling price remains constant, find out approximately what percentage of the selling price is the profit?

    &SP = CP + Profit = 100 + 320 = 420 \\ \\ \\

    &\text{If the cost increases by 25%, New CP} = \dfrac{125}{100} \times 100 = 125 \\

    \\ &\text{Selling Price is constant, hence New SP} = 420 \\

    \\ &Profit = SP – CP = 420 – 125 = 295 \\

    \\ &\text{Required Percentage }= \dfrac{295}{420}\times 100

    \\ \\&= \dfrac{2950}{42}

    \\ \\&= \dfrac{1475}{21}

    \\ \\&\approx 70%

    \end{align}$MF#%

    6. The percentage profit earned by selling an item for Rs. 1920 is equal to the percentage loss incurred by selling the same item for Rs. 1280. At what price should the item be sold to make 25% profit?

    A. Insufficient Data

    B. Rs. 3000

    C. Rs. 2000

    D. Rs. 2200

    View Answer
    | Discuss

    Here is the answer and explanation

    Answer : Option C

    Explanation :

    $MF#%\begin{align}

    &\text{Let CP = x} \\ \\

    &\text{Percentage profit earned by selling an item for Rs. 1920} \\ \\

    &= \dfrac{SP-CP}{CP} \times 100 \\ \\

    &= \dfrac{1920-x}{x} \times 100 \\ \\ \\



    &\text{Percentage loss incurred by selling the same item for Rs. 1280}\\ \\

    &= \dfrac{CP-SP}{CP} \times 100 \\ \\

    &= \dfrac{x-1280}{x} \times 100 \\ \\ \\



    &\text{Given that Percentage profit earned by selling an item for Rs. 1920} = \\

    &\text{Percentage loss incurred by selling the same item for Rs. 1280} \\ \\

    &\Rightarrow \dfrac{1920-x}{x} \times 100 = \dfrac{x-1280}{x} \times 100\\ \\

    &\Rightarrow \dfrac{1920-x}{x} = \dfrac{x-1280}{x} \\ \\

    &\Rightarrow 1920 – x = x – 1280 \\ \\

    &\Rightarrow 2x = 1920 + 1280 = 3200 \\ \\

    &\Rightarrow x = \dfrac{3200}{2} \\ \\

    &= 1600 \\ \\ \\

    &\text{Required Selling Price} = CP \times \dfrac{125}{100} \\ \\

    &= 1600 \times \dfrac{125}{100} = 1600 \times \dfrac{5}{4} \\ \\

    &= 400 \times 5 = 2000

    \end{align}$MF#%

    9. Murali purchased 20 dozens of toys at the rate of Rs. 375 per dozen. He sold each one of them at the rate of Rs. 33. Find out his profit percentage.

    A. 3.5

    B. 5.6

    C. 4.1

    D. 3.4

    View Answer
    | Discuss

    Here is the answer and explanation

    Answer : Option B

    Explanation :

    $MF#%\begin{align}

    &\text{CP of 1 toy} = \dfrac{375}{12} \\ \\

    &\text{SP of 1 toy} = 33 \\ \\

    &\text{Profit} = SP - CP = 33 - \dfrac{375}{12} \\ \\

    &\text{Profit}\% = \dfrac{Profit}{CP} \times 100 = \dfrac{\left(33 - \dfrac{375}{12}\right) }{\dfrac{375}{12}} \times 100 \\ \\

    &= \left(33 \times \dfrac{12}{375} - 1 \right) \times 100 = \left(33 \times \dfrac{4}{125} - 1 \right) \times 100 \\ \\

    &= \dfrac{7}{125} \times 100 = \dfrac{7}{5} \times 4 = \dfrac{28}{5} = 5\dfrac{3}{5}\% = 5.6\%

    \end{align}$MF#%

    12. When an item is sold for Rs. 18,700, the owner loses 15%. At what price should that plot be sold to get a gain of 15%?

    A. Rs. 25100

    B. Rs. 24200

    C. Rs. 25300

    D. Rs. 21200

    View Answer
    | Discuss

    Here is the answer and explanation

    Answer : Option C

    Explanation :

    $MF#%\begin{align}

    &\text{When an item is sold for Rs. 18,700, the owner loses 15%} \\ \\

    &\Rightarrow \text{SP = 18700 and Loss} = 15\% \\ \\

    &\Rightarrow CP = \dfrac{100}{100 - Loss\%} \times SP = \dfrac{100}{100 - 15} \times 18700 = \dfrac{100}{85} \times 18700 \\ \\\\



    &\text{To get a gain of 15%, SP }= \dfrac{100 + Gain\%}{100} \times CP = \dfrac{100 + 15}{100} \times CP = \dfrac{115}{100} \times CP \\ \\

    &= \dfrac{115}{100} \times \dfrac{100}{85} \times 18700 = \dfrac{115}{85} \times 18700 = \dfrac{23}{17} \times 18700 \\ \\

    &= 23 \times 1100 = 25300

    \end{align} $MF#%

    14. A shopkeeper sells one radio for Rs. 840 at a gain of 20% and another for Rs. 960 at a loss of 4%. What is his total gain or loss percentage?

    A. $MF#%5\%$MF#%

    B. $MF#%6\%$MF#%

    C. $MF#%6\dfrac{12}{17}\%$MF#%

    D. $MF#%5\dfrac{15}{17}\%$MF#%

    View Answer
    | Discuss

    Here is the answer and explanation

    Answer : Option D

    Explanation :

    $MF#%\begin{align}

    &\text{SP of 1st Radio} = 840 \\ \\

    &\text{Gain} = 20\% \\ \\

    &\text{CP of 1st Radio} = \dfrac{100}{\left(100 + Gain\%\right)} \times SP = \dfrac{100}{\left(100 + 20\right)} \times 840 = 100 \times 7 = 700 \\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\

    &\text{SP of 2nd Radio} = 960 \\ \\

    &\text{Loss} = 4\% \\ \\

    &\text{CP of 2nd Radio} = \dfrac{100}{\left(100 - Loss\%\right)} \times SP = \dfrac{100}{\left(100 - 4\right)} \times 960 = 100 \times 10 = 1000 \\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\

    &\text{Total CP} = 700 + 1000 = 1700 \\ \\

    &\text{Total SP} = 840 + 960 = 1800 \\ \\

    &\text{Total Gain} = SP - CP = 1800 - 1700 = 100 \\ \\

    &\text{Gain}\% = \dfrac{Gain}{CP} \times 100 = \dfrac{100}{1700} \times 100 = \dfrac{100}{17} = 5\dfrac{15}{17}



    \end{align} $MF#%
